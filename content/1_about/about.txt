Intro: A Dutch software development student who enjoys building practical tools, clean UIs, and little automations that remove repetitive work. The focus is on writing code that feels understandable, maintainable, and just a bit over-engineered in the right places.

----

Journey:

I grew up curious about how things work, especially anything with buttons, screens, or a settings menu. That curiosity slowly turned into a habit of **taking things apart**, breaking software, and then figuring out how to fix it again.

### Studying software development

Choosing software development as a study path was a natural move. It combines problem-solving, logic, and creativity, and it rewards you for being stubborn in a good way. At school I moved from *"just make it work"* to thinking about structure, readability, and how other developers will experience the code.

### Falling in love with the web

The web became my main focus: HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and PHP with Kirby CMS for fast, content-driven sites. I enjoy building things that feel **simple on the surface**, even when there's a lot of logic and automation running underneath.

### Real projects, real lessons

To really learn, I started building tools and websites that other people could actually use. Each project is a small case study: what went well, what broke in production, and what I can automate or refactor next time.

### What I'm working towards

Right now I'm focused on sharpening my full-stack web skills, building more reliable automation, and creating a portfolio that reflects how I think as a developer. The goal is to be able to take an idea from rough sketch to **deployed, maintainable software** — and have fun along the way.
